# Demo Patient: Gerald Morrison — The Case That Wins

## The Story (for the demo video)

Gerald Morrison is a 71-year-old retired steelworker from Cleveland, scheduled for 
**elective abdominal aortic aneurysm (AAA) repair** in 3 weeks.

His surgeon ordered a pre-operative clearance. In a typical hospital, a resident would 
spend 30-45 minutes reviewing his chart, manually checking each medication, calculating 
risk scores by hand, and writing up the clearance note.

**PreOp Intelligence does it in 30 seconds.**

## What Makes This Case Win

Every single tool fires with dramatic, clinically significant findings:

### Risk Scores (Tool 2)
- **ASA IV** — severe systemic disease, constant threat to life
- **RCRI 5/6** — >15% major cardiac event risk (5 of 6 risk factors positive)
- **Caprini VTE: very high** — requires extended prophylaxis
- **STOP-BANG 7/8** — high OSA risk, needs CPAP post-op

### Medication Flags (Tool 3)  
- **WARFARIN** — CRITICAL: hold 5 days pre-op, check INR day before, bridge with LMWH
- **Insulin glargine** — reduce 50-80% night before
- **Lisinopril** — hold morning of surgery (hypotension risk)
- **Furosemide** — hold morning of surgery
- **Aspirin** — continue (cardiac indication)
- **Metoprolol** — continue (protective, withdrawal risk)

### Lab Alerts (Tool 4)
- Hemoglobin 10.1 g/dL — **ANEMIA** (transfusion threshold for surgery)
- Creatinine 2.1 — **KIDNEY FAILURE** (affects drug dosing, contrast risk)
- BNP 520 — **DECOMPENSATED CHF** (may need optimization before surgery)
- HbA1c 7.8% — **UNCONTROLLED DIABETES**
- INR 2.6 — **SUPRATHERAPEUTIC** (bleeding risk if not managed)
- Glucose 158 — **HYPERGLYCEMIA**

### Anesthesia Concerns (Tool 5)
- **HIGH airway risk** — BMI 36.2, neck 43cm, OSA on CPAP
- **Penicillin allergy (anaphylaxis)** — need alternative antibiotic prophylaxis
- **CHF** — arterial line, fluid monitoring
- **COPD risk** (if present)
- Difficult intubation anticipated

### Escalation Flags (Tool 6 — full report)
- HIGH CARDIAC RISK — cardiology consult recommended
- ASA IV — high perioperative mortality risk  
- CRITICAL MEDICATION MANAGEMENT — Warfarin
- DIFFICULT AIRWAY ANTICIPATED
- VERY HIGH VTE RISK
- MISSING/EXPIRED LABS (if applicable)
